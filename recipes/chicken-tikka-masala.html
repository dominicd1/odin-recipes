Chicken Tikka Masala

About the Dish

There’s something almost magical about Chicken Tikka Masala—a dish that brings the bold heat of Indian spices and the smooth comfort of a creamy tomato sauce into one glorious, flame-kissed harmony. Tender chunks of yogurt-marinated chicken, charred to smoky perfection, swim in a luxurious, velvety curry that clings lovingly to every bite of basmati rice or naan. It’s fiery but balanced, rich without being heavy, and aromatic enough to make your neighbors “accidentally” stop by. Whether you’re cooking for comfort or flexing for friends, this dish is the showstopper your kitchen was built for.

Each spoonful is a love letter to the perfect balance—heat softened by cream, brightness mellowed by smoke, spices that don’t shout but sing in harmony. The chicken isn’t just cooked, it’s transformed: marinated overnight in tangy yogurt and warm spices, then seared or grilled until the edges char like the memory of a tandoor. And that sauce? Oh, that sauce. It’s a slow-simmered blend of ginger, garlic, tomatoes, and heavy cream that coats your tongue like velvet and begs to be mopped up with warm naan. This isn’t just comfort food—it’s conversation-stopping, phone-down, eyes-closed food.

Ingredients

Chicken Marinade

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch piece ginger, grated

Masala Sauce

  • 2 tbsp oil or butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional, for heat)
  • 1 (15 oz) can crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream (or use coconut milk for dairy-free)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)

Cooking Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken
    • In a bowl, combine:
      • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
      • 1 tbsp lemon juice
      • 2 tsp garam masala
      • 1 tsp ground cumin
      • 1 tsp paprika
      • 1/2 tsp turmeric
      • 1 tsp salt
      • 2 cloves garlic, minced
      • 1 inch piece ginger, grated
    • Add 1 lb chicken chunks and mix well.
    • Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to overnight
  2. Cook the Chicken
    • Heat a pan over medium-high heat with a drizzle of oil.
    • Add the marinated chicken pieces (no extra marinade).
    • Cook until browned and cooked through, about 5–7 minutes.
    • Set aside on a plate.
  3. Make the Masala Sauce
    • In the same pan, add 2 tbsp oil or butter.
    • Add 1 chopped onion and cook until soft and golden, about 5 minutes.
    • Stir in 2 minced garlic cloves and 1 inch grated ginger.
    • Add:
      • 1 tbsp garam masala
      • 1 tsp cumin
      • 1/2 tsp paprika
      • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional, for heat)
    • Cook the spices for 1-2 minutes to toast them.
  4. Add Tomatoes and Simmer
    • Pour in 1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes.
    • Stir and let it simmer for 10 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly.
    • Season with a pinch of salt, to taste.
  5. Finish with Cream and Chicken
    • Stir in 3/4 cup heavy cream (or coconut milk).
    • Add the cooked chicken back to the pan.
    • Simmer everything together for 5–10 minutes until creamy and bubbling.
  6. Serve and Enjoy
    • Garnish with fresh chopped cilantro (optional).
    • Serve with steamed rice, naan, or both.
