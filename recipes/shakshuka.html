Shakshuka

About the Dish

Shakshuka is what happens when your breakfast decides it wants to be a full-on event. Eggs are gently poached in a bubbling skillet of spiced tomatoes, sweet onions, and peppers, with garlic and cumin swirling through the steam like morning incense. The whites set just firm, the yolks stay gloriously runny, and the whole thing practically begs for a crusty slice of bread to scoop it up. Whether you’re waking up slow or hosting brunch with flair, shakshuka brings bold flavor, no stress, and major comfort in every bite.

At its core, shakshuka is beautifully simple: pantry staples turned into a Middle Eastern masterpiece. The sauce simmers low and slow, thick with crushed tomatoes, sautéed onions, garlic, and bell peppers, blooming with warm spices like cumin, paprika, and a touch of chili for subtle heat. The eggs rest in cozy little craters, barely disturbed as they set in the spiced warmth. Every spoonful gives you silky egg, rich tomato, and just the right kick of flavor. It’s rustic, it’s one-pan, and it never fails to impress—whether it’s breakfast, lunch, or that 10pm “what’s-in-the-fridge” moment.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), chopped
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/4 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • 1 (15 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4–6 large eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, for garnish (optional)
  • Bread, for serving (toasted or fresh)

Cooking Steps

  1. Sauté the Veggies
    • Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
    • Add 1 chopped onion and 1 chopped bell pepper. Cook until soft, about 5–7 minutes.
    • Stir in 2–3 minced garlic cloves. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Add Spices and Tomatoes
    • Add 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp paprika, and 1/4 tsp chili flakes (optional). Stir for 1 minute.
    • Pour in 1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    • Simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.
  3. Add the Eggs
    • Make little wells in the sauce with a spoon.
    • Crack 4–6 eggs into the wells, one at a time.
    • Cover the pan with a lid and cook for 5–8 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but yolks are still runny (or longer if you prefer them firmer).
  4. Finish and Serve
    • Sprinkle with fresh parsley and cilantro if you like
    • Serve hot with crusty bread for dipping
